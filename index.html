Biography

Victoria Hanna is an Israeli singer and performer known for her unique fusion of ancient Hebrew texts, contemporary music, and experimental vocal techniques. Born in 1983 in Jerusalem, Hanna developed a passion for music from an early age. She studied voice, classical music, and linguistics, which laid the foundation for her innovative approach to music and language.

Hanna's artistic style is characterized by her ability to seamlessly blend traditional Jewish texts, such as the Hebrew Bible and Kabbalistic writings, with modern beats and melodies. Her compositions often explore themes of spirituality, feminism, and the power of language. Using her voice as a versatile instrument, she employs a wide range of vocal techniques, including guttural sounds, throat singing, and vocal percussion, creating mesmerizing and otherworldly performances.

With her boundary-pushing artistry, Victoria Hanna has garnered international acclaim and has performed in prestigious venues and festivals around the world. She has released several albums, including "Victoria Hanna" which showcase her exceptional vocal skills and captivating storytelling abilities. Through her music, Hanna challenges conventions, breaks down barriers, and invites listeners on a thought-provoking journey that bridges the ancient and the contemporary. Her work continues to inspire and redefine the possibilities of music and language in the modern era.
